Who we recruit

Undergraduates

You take on a defined part of a study with close mentorship, and learn data handling, the literature, and how a question becomes a result. Sustained contributors earn authorship.

Medical students

You run your own clinical or computational project from question to submission, and present at AAOS and allied meetings, building a focused body of published work during medical school.

Orthopaedic residents and fellows

Bring a clinical question and use the lab's existing data, tooling, and mentorship to pursue it without building infrastructure from scratch. Work spans biomechanics, robotics, and large-database studies.

Postdocs and collaborators

If you work in machine learning, biomechanics, causal inference, or related fields, we have clinical problems and the data to pursue them. We host visiting scholars and maintain collaborations across institutions.

What we value

We care most about initiative. People do well here when they bring their own ideas and carry them through to publication. The technical skills (programming, statistics, imaging, mechanics) can be learned in the lab.

How to apply

Read through the research and find what you would want to work on, then send the short application form. If there is a fit, we will talk it through and settle on the right project.
